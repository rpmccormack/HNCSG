Professionalism & Ethics

The Professionalism and Ethics unit in HNC focuses on policy, legislation, company compliance and professional bodies.  Below I've detailed the key professional bodies and legislation that you need to know for the HNC graded unit exam.  

Policies and Legislation - Company Compliance

Copyright, Design and Patents Act 1988

Copyright law protects original work, such as photo, video, text and games.
  • Copyright applies automatically
  • It is illegal to copy protected work without permission
  • The creator can apply for a patent but this costs money
  • Copy right law doesn't apply to ideas
    •

    Computer Misuse Act 1990

    The Computer Misuse Act makes it illegal to:
  • Access data on computer systems without permission
  • Access material without permission with intent to commit a crime
  • Modify or delete data without permission
    •

    Data Protection Act 2018

    The Data Proection Act has 7 principles that are set out on the ICO website.  The Data Protection Act enforces how personal data should be handled by organisations. 
  • Data must be processed lawfully and fairly
  • It must be collected and processed for specified and legitimate purposes
  • Data must be relevant and limited to what is necessary to te stipulated purposes (data minimisation)
  • It must be accurate and kept up to date
  • Data should be stored for no longer tan necessary
  • It should be processed with appropriate security and protected from loss
  • The controller of the data is responsible and accountable
    •

    Consumer protection legislation

    Consumer Protection Legislation is intended to protect consumers from bad quality products and poor business practice.

    The Consumer Rights Act (2015) This legislation protects consumers from unfair business practices. It applies to transactions between buyer and seller and includes digital products. This covers:
  • Returns
  • Replacement and repair
  • delivery
  • The product or service
  • Cooling off period for distance selling (normally 14-days)
    •

    Goods need to be described accurately, fit for purpose and of a satisfactory quality. With regards to services, they need to be delivered with reasonable care and skill. Verbal and written details the seller provides are binding. The service must be provided in a reasonable time or an agreement has to be made about that. If the seller doesn't agree a price beforehand then the price must be reasonable.

    Returns
    Under the Consumer Rights Act if a product is faulty or not as described the customer can return it within 30 days (not including digital products). If a product fails within 6 months the fault is considered to have been there at the time of purchase. If this occurs after 6 months then it's on the consumer to prove the fault was there at the time of purchase.

    The Consumer Protection Act (1987)
    The Consumer Protection Act mades the manufacturer liable for any damage caused by manufacturing defects and is intended to ensure products are safe for consumers.

    Health and Safety Act 1974

    The Health and Safety Act (1974) protects the safety and welfare of workers. Under this legislation employers must ensure that:
  • Their Health and Safety Policy is updated
  • Staff receive health and safety training(fire drills, using equipment safely etc)
  • That they provide appropriate safety equipment to employees
  • The working enviroment is safe
  • First aid facilities are available
    •

    Disability Discrimination Act 1995

  • Makes it unlawful to discriminate against someone on the basis of a disability in regards to goods and services, work, education, or transport.
    •

    Waste Electronic and Electrical Equipment Regulations 2006 (WEEE)

    This legislation is intended to reduce the amount of electrical waste that goes to landfill. It has been put in place to ensure electrical waste is appropriately disposed of once it reaches the end of its use.
  • Equipment purchased before 2005, it's the company/individual who is responsible for recycling it.
  • After 2006 it is the responsibility of te manufacturer/distributer. They collect and recycle the equipment.
    •

    Professional Bodies

  • BCS (British Computer Society).
  • IET (Institution for Engineering and Technology).
  • IAP (Institute for Analysts and Programmers).
  • IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers).
  • Know the benefits Members must adhere to codes of conduct, keep up to date with latest news, have access to continuous proessional development, legal advice, networking opportunities, etc
  • Know the professions which are most suited to each professional body, i.e. a networking technician might be a member of the IEEE, a programmer might be in the IAP, etc.
    •

    Codes of conduct

    Be familiar with the normal elements included in the codes of conduct for professional bodies. A code of conduct is a set of files that outline the responsibilities for individuals, parties and organisations. Related concepts include ethical, honor and moral codes and laws.

    Here are a couple of examples that could be included in a code of ethics:

    Members should only do work that they are qualified to undertake. Don't agree to do a job in an area that you know you aren't fully qualified for, be transparent about your abilities when selling your services.

    Members should act with integrity and professionalism towards other members and IT professionals and show respect to everyone.
